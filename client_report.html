Client / Developer Team Project Report

Client on the Phillips site, lead developer on the Bowerbank site, and junior developer on the Gurr site.

Client Report

Site: wdd130-phillips

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I spent some time looking up images just online like Google Images. However, I wasn't satisfied with some of the images I wanted and couldn't find, so I had Gemini create a couple of images for me.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

I communicated well with my developer actually. Me, James Gurr, and Logan Bowerbank are all in a Discord chat where we share ideas about our websites and each fulfill our roles in that chat. There was never any issues with communication.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Yes, for what I asked him to do, it was perfect. I'm sure I could've asked him to do more, but I didn't, so that was a fail on my end.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

I want to be more engaged with the repo about the changes made and to be actively engaged in the visual aspects that are taking place in the website. I want to look through the code and ask myself if I would do that.

Lead Developer Report

Site: wdd130-bowerbank

How did you manage your development team and communicate the tasks for your junior devs?

I just had James Gurr as my junior dev and he did a great job taking orders about the website and completing tasks. I laid out which pages I would do and which page I would give him to be in charge over. We once again used Discord to communicate, which worked well.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

Like I said above, we used Discord to communicate, which was the perfect platform for us since we all are on there much. There wasn't any issues.

How did you handle publishing your client's site and sharing it with the client?

I'm pretty familiar with GitHub but not GitHub Pages, so that took me a second to learn how to publish the site. Once we got the repo up and going, it got easier. Every time we made changes, we would message the client in Discord to have him check our work.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

Like said above, it was confusing at first, but I loved this project because I learned more about GitHub Desktop as well as GitHub Pages, which was a good tool for me to learn.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

I think the beginning week was the hardest part. Simply just getting the Discord setup and making sure all of us understood what our roles were and what we were even trying to accomplish. Past week 1 was a lot easier because we all knew what was going on and what we wanted and needed to accomplish.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I enjoyed working with others who are learning as well. I also enjoyed the aspect of using GitHub on our own, which requires learning and researching on our own. I also love the idea of creating a website on our own as well.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I would be better prepared for the first week so that I can spend more time designing the plan for my website as the client.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

Yes. I feel as though I have learned a lot of valuable lessons through this project. Repeating what I have been saying, I have loved learning more about GitHub. Knowing how to use Git better I think is a very important skill for real-world projects in the future with work. So, I am grateful that I was able to learn new skills from this assignment.
